Shahi Paneer: The Crown Jewel of Mughalai Cuisine

Description

Shahi Paneer is not just a dish—it's an experience, a luxurious journey through the royal kitchens of India's glorious past. Originating from the rich culinary traditions of the Mughal Empire, the word “Shahi” literally translates to “royal,” and paneer is the beloved Indian cottage cheese. Together, they create a dish that's revered across the Indian subcontinent for its creamy texture, aromatic spices, and indulgent flavor. At its core, Shahi Paneer is made by simmering soft cubes of paneer in a silky, rich gravy that typically includes tomatoes, onions, ginger, garlic, cream, and a generous mix of Indian spices. But what truly sets this dish apart is the use of ground cashews or almonds, which lend a nutty sweetness and luxurious thickness to the gravy, giving it that signature royal mouthfeel. A splash of cream or milk is added at the end to elevate its richness and give it a beautiful, velvety finish.

The Flavour Profile

Shahi Paneer isn't meant to be fiery or overpoweringly spicy. Instead, it carries a mild, aromatic profile—deep and complex, with hints of sweetness from the nuts and a subtle heat from spices like garam masala, cardamom, and cumin. It's the perfect balance of savory and sweet, rich but not overwhelming, indulgent yet comforting.

Royal Roots

Back in the day, dishes like Shahi Paneer were served to royalty in grand feasts, garnished with slivers of gold or silver leaf (varak) and paired with lavish breads like naan or kulcha. While the modern home cook might skip the edible bling, the essence of opulence still remains in every bite. Even today, Shahi Paneer is a star dish in weddings, celebrations, and high-end Indian restaurants.

How it's served

Shahi Paneer is best enjoyed with buttery naan, tandoori roti, or steamed basmati rice. The rich gravy clings to every grain or piece of bread, making every mouthful satisfying and flavorful. Some like to top it with fresh coriander leaves, a swirl of cream, or even fried onions for added texture and depth.

Fun Fact

In many Indian households, Shahi Paneer is a weekend special—a reward after a long week, or the centerpiece of a Sunday lunch with the fam. It's the kind of dish that brings people to the table, eager for seconds (and thirds).

Ingrediants

Main Ingrediants

  • Paneer(Cottage Cheese) - 200 to 250g(cubed)
  • Onions - 2 medium-sized (roughly chopped)
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium-sized (roughly chopped or pureed)
  • Cashew nuts - 10 to 15 (soaked for 10 mins, then blended)
  • Ginger - 1 inch (grated or chopped)
  • Garlic - 4-5 cloves (chopped)
  • Green chili - 1 (optional, for mild heat)

For the Rich Gravy

  • Fresh cream (malai or heavy cream) - 2 to 3 tablespoons
  • Milk - ¼ cup (optional, for adjusting consistency)
  • Butter or Ghee - 2 tablespoons (for flavor)
  • Oil - 1 tablespoon (to sauté onions/tomatoes)

Spices

  • Turmeric powder (haldi) - ¼ teaspoon
  • Red chili powder - ½ to 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Coriander powder (dhania) - 1 teaspoon
  • Garam masala - ½ teaspoon
  • Cardamom pods (elaichi) - 2 (or ¼ tsp cardamom powder)
  • Cumin seeds (jeera) - ½ teaspoon
  • Bay leaf (tej patta) - 1
  • Cinnamon stick - 1 small piece (optional)
  • Cloves (laung) - 2 (optional)
  • Salt - to taste
  • Sugar or honey - ½ to 1 teaspoon (for balancing the flavor)

Optional Garnish

  • Fresh coriander leaves - chopped
  • Crushed kasuri methi (dried fenugreek) - ½ tsp (adds a nice aroma)
  • Silver leaf (varak) - only if you're feeling extra royal

Steps

Step 1: Prep the cashew-onion base

  1. Boil or sauté these in a bit of oil/water for 5-7 mins until soft:
    • 2 chopped onions
    • 2 tomatoes
    • 10-12 cashews
    • 1 green chili (optional)
  2. Let it cool, then blend into a smooth paste. This is your creamy masala base.

Step 2: Cook the masala

  1. In a pan, heat 1 tbsp oil + 1 tbsp butter
  2. Add:
    • 1 bay leaf
    • 2 cardamom pods
    • Small cinnamon stick (optional)
    • ½ tsp cumin seeds
  3. Once aromatic, add the cashew-onion-tomato paste.
  4. Cook on medium heat for 5-7 mins, stirring so it doesn't stick.

Step 3: Spice it up

Add the spices

  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • ½-1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste

    • Mix well and cook for 3-4 mins until the oil starts to separate

Step 4: Bring in the royalty (Paneer and Cream)

  1. Add ¼ cup water or milk to adjust thickness.
  2. Add 200-250g paneer cubes and simmer for 3-4 minutes.
  3. Stir in 2-3 tbsp fresh cream for that royal richness.
  4. Optional: crush and sprinkle ½ tsp kasuri methi (dried fenugreek) for aroma.

    5. Step 5: Garnish and Serve

    • Top with a swirl of cream
    • Garnish with fresh coriandar leaves
    • Serve hot with naan, roti, or jeera rice
